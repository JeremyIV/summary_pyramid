9. **Jonah's physical discomfort in sensuous language**: "all dressed and dusty as he is, Jonah throws himself into his berth" and "after sore wrestlings in his berth, Jonah's prodigy of ponderous misery drags him drowning down to sleep."

10. **Ishmael's meditation on male intimacy**: "No man prefers to sleep two in a bed. In fact, you would a good deal rather not sleep with your own brother... And when it comes to sleeping with an unknown stranger... then your objections indefinitely multiply." This extended reflection reveals both anxiety and fascination with male physical closeness.

The novel establishes an intensely homosocial world of whaling where men live in close quarters and form deep bonds. The relationship between Ishmael and Queequeg develops quickly from fear to intimate companionship, described in language that modern readers might interpret as homoerotic. The text consistently juxtaposes Queequeg's "otherness" with the growing comfort and intimacy between the two men, creating subtext that could be read as expressing same-sex attraction or deep homosocial bonding typical of 19th-century maritime literature.
